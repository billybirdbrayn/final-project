About the Agility App

Once a dog reaches the Master Standard and Master Jumper with Weaves classes, the dog begins to accure points toward their championship. This title is refered to as the MACH (Master Agility Champion). In order to earn this title, a dog must earn 750 master points. The purpose of this app is to keep a running tally of master points.

Often times a contestent is at a dog show and the American Kennel Club has not had an opportunity to process previously earned points. Thus, this app will allow you to keep your own tally so that you do not miss celebrating your MACH victory. When that sought after MACH is earned, the team (dog and handler) take a victory lap in the ring!

Important Rules of the Game


The tire is one of many types of jumps in agility. It is often used at the start of a course. In addition to clearing a jump, a dog must not knock a bar over. That is an automatic disqualification. This is Ellie clearing the tire.

The teeter is part of the Standard ring. When the dog ascends up as well as descends down the teeter, the dog must make contact with the yellow zone. Dogs are not permitted to jump off the teeter. Billy loved the teeter!

Billy our Team Leader

Weavepoles are also in both the Standard and Jumpers ring. They happen to be the most difficult skill to master. In the master ring, there are twelve poles to a set. The dog must make the appropriate entry and weave around each pole.
